How Transparent Milestones Protect Both Customers & Traders

BRISK Editorial Team Published in Trust & Safety
July 20, 2026 6 min read

For decades, home service contracts have suffered from a fundamental lack of trust. Customers worry about paying large deposits upfront before seeing completed work, while traders fear non-payment after investing labor and material costs.

The traditional local trade workflow forces both parties to take huge financial risks. However, digitally governed milestone frameworks are completely changing this dynamic. By breaking projects down into clear, agreed-upon phases, BRISK eliminates guesswork and aligns expectations from day one.

The Problem With Traditional Trade Payments

In a typical unmonitored arrangement, one party must compromise first. If the homeowner pays a 50% deposit, they have little recourse if the contractor delays or walks away. Conversely, if the contractor performs 100% of the labor without escrow or milestone tracking, they risk prolonged client disputes over subjective preferences.

"Trust isn't built on handshakes alone anymore—it's built on transparent agreements where every dollar is backed by documented, verifiable progress."

How Milestone Governance Works in BRISK

When a trader submits a proposal on BRISK, the estimate is broken into distinct phases. For example, a complete bathroom renovation might include:

  • Phase 1: Demolition & Site Preparation — Materials delivered and old fixtures removed safely.
  • Phase 2: Rough Plumbing & Electrical Wiring — Core infrastructure inspected and signed off.
  • Phase 3: Tiling & Fixture Installation — Main aesthetic and functional installation completed.
  • Phase 4: Final Inspection & Handover — Final walkthrough, testing, and full sign-off.

Protected Escrow Account

Funds for each milestone are held securely in escrow. The customer knows their money is safe, and the trader knows the funds are reserved and guaranteed upon completion.

Key Benefits for Homeowners

Homeowners gain full peace of mind. You never pay for unrendered work. If an issue arises during Phase 2, you address it directly before funds for Phase 3 are unlocked. Additionally, photo evidence and chat history are permanently tied to each milestone record.

Key Benefits for Skilled Traders

For contractors, milestone tracking solves cash flow bottlenecks. Instead of waiting until the entire project is completed to receive payment, funds are released incrementally as each phase is completed. This ensures continuous cash flow for purchasing materials and paying crew members on time.
